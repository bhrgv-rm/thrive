Study in the USA

Thrive Overseas – Launch Your Career in the City of Innovation and Luxury

Why Study in USA?

  • World-Class Education System: Home to Harvard, MIT, Stanford, and other top-ranking global universities.
  • Cultural Diversity: A multicultural society that enhances global exposure and networking.
  • Scholarships and Financial Aid: Universities offer funding to international students to ease financial burden.
  • Flexible Curriculum: Customize your academic path, change majors, and explore interdisciplinary programs.
  • Post-Study Work Opportunities: Work in the USA after graduation through OPT and H-1B pathways.
  • Strong Career Prospects: Globally respected degrees and strong ties to industry leaders open job opportunities.

Life in USA

  • Cultural Adjustment: Diverse and inclusive environment with cultural support networks.
  • Accommodation: On-campus housing and off-campus rentals are widely available.
  • Healthcare: Health insurance is mandatory; universities often offer affordable plans.

Types of USA Study Visas

  • F1 Visa: For full-time academic programs such as undergraduate and postgraduate degrees. Spouses and children apply for F2.
  • J1 Visa: For exchange programs, internships, and cultural exchange. Dependents apply for J2.
  • M1 Visa: For vocational or non-academic programs like culinary arts or mechanics. Dependents apply for M2.

Additional Work Opportunities

  • Curricular Practical Training (CPT): Work during study as part of the curriculum. Requires school authorization.
  • Optional Practical Training (OPT): Work in your field of study after graduation for up to 12–36 months.
  • H-1B Visa: A non-immigrant work visa allowing long-term employment in the U.S. after OPT.
